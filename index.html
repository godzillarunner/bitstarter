Your are in a restaurant or grabbing a byte to eat. You wonder how healthy is the food on your plate. How many calories and the nutritional value? You could try to decipher the menu, but what you have on your plate is not exactly what's covered on the menu. Instead, pick up your smart phone, snap a photo of the food on your plate, and send the picture to platesnap.com. We will analyze the food and give health information about what's on your plate. The information includes calorie count, nutrition, food warnings, etc. Give us some more information and we will include reviews and comments from other users. You will also have the opportunity to submit your personal review of the items on your plate.

We use Plate-IQ technology developed at U.C. Berkeley to estimate the caloric content of the food on your plate. Sophisticated image processing programs, used by the government for spying, enable us to standardize the photos regardless of your expertise as a photographer. We then combine the information obtained from the photo with the information in our large and ever expanding database to give you the health information you need. Why wonder if that food is good for you when you can find out for sure?

FAQ
